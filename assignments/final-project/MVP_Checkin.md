ECE 464: Databases – Final Project MVP (Midway Check-in)
The Objective
As we cross the halfway mark of the semester, it is time for a reality check on your capstone projects. The Minimum Viable Product (MVP) check-in is not about having a finished, polished application; it is about proving that your foundation is solid, your core infrastructure is wired up, and you are actively building.

Because you are utilizing AI to accelerate your development, avoiding the "end-of-semester cram" is critical to bypass rate limits and API exhaustion. This check-in ensures you have a functional baseline that we can expand upon for the final presentation during exam week.

Deliverables
Please submit a 1-page write-up (PDF or Markdown) addressing the sections below, along with a link to your repository.

1. The Abstract (What are you building?)
Provide a concise, 2–3 sentence description of your project. If your core idea has evolved since your initial proposal, state clearly what the application does now and who it is for.

2. Current State of the Application (What actually works?)
List the minimal set of features that are currently operational. I am looking for proof of an initial state. For example:

Is your database deployed and accessible?

Is user authentication (e.g., Supabase) successfully gating access?

Can users perform basic CRUD operations on your core tables?

Is the frontend successfully talking to your API?

3. Schema & Architecture Check-in
Briefly describe the current state of your database schema.

Roughly how many tables are currently implemented via your migration system (e.g., Alembic)?

Have you run into any early relational bottlenecks or indexing questions?

4. The Pivot (Deviations from the Proposal)
Startups pivot, and your project likely will too. Detail any significant deviations you have made from your original proposal.

Did you swap out a piece of the recommended tech stack?

Did you abandon your original "Complexity Component" for a different technical challenge?

Explain why these deviations were taken and how you handled them. (Pivots are not only acceptable but encouraged if they lead to a better or more feasible product).

5. Blockers & Next Steps
What are the biggest technical hurdles between your current MVP and your final production-ready app? Identify specific areas where you are currently stuck or anticipate needing help (e.g., "I am struggling to map the external API data into my current schema" or "I am having trouble configuring background jobs").

6. Code Access
Include a link to your GitHub repository. Ensure it is either public or that I have been granted access so I can review your schema, your migrations, and the overall structure of your code.

The 1-on-1 Review
After reviewing your 1-page submission and your codebase, we will have a brief 1-on-1 chat. We will use this time to unblock any technical hurdles, review your schema design, and ensure your "Complexity Component" is scoped correctly for the final few weeks of the semester.
