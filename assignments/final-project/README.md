### **Core Project Requirements**
This project represents **40% of your final grade**. To be successful, your application must include:

* **Individual Effort:** You are the founder and lead architect.
* **Database Design:** A baseline of **10 to 15 tables**. This is a loose guideline to ensure your schema supports meaningful, non-trivial interactions.
* **Authentication:** A full user account system with secure login and data ownership.
* **Cloud Deployment:** Your app must be live and accessible via public URLs.
* **Database Migrations:** Manual SQL "fixes" are not allowed. You must use a formal migration system (e.g., **Alembic**).
* **External Integration:** At least one major third-party software integration (e.g., **Stripe** for payments, **Twilio** for SMS, **Square**, etc.).
* **The Data Seed:** We will build webscrapers later in the semester to seed your database.

### **The $\ast$ (Complexity) Component**
To meet the graduate-level standard, your project must include one "non-obvious" technical challenge that goes beyond standard CRUD operations. We will discuss your specific choice individually, but examples include:
* **Hybrid Data Systems:** Using a NoSQL or Vector database alongside Postgres for semantic search.
* **Concurrency & Performance:** Implementing multi-threading, batch processing, or complex background jobs.
* **Domain-Specific Logic:** Backend engines involving heavy mathematical modeling or specialized scheduling queries.

### **Progress & Expectations**
* **Public GitHub:** You are required to create a **public GitHub repository immediately**. I will be monitoring these to see your progress in real-time.
* **Weekly Stand-ups:** Every week, we will do a 1-minute "around the room" update. Be prepared to show what you've shipped and where you are blocked.
